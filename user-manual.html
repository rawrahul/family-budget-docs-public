Complete guide to all features in Family Budget Tracker. Learn how to make the most of your budgeting experience.

üè† Dashboard

Your dashboard is the central hub showing your financial overview for the current pay cycle.

Dashboard Tabs

The dashboard has three main tabs:

  • Overview: Financial summary, AI insights, and spending breakdown
  • Savings Goals: Quick view of your physical and virtual goals
  • Transactions: Search, filter, and manage transactions

Key Metrics

  • Income: Total money received this pay cycle
  • Expenses: Total spent (excluding transfers and refunds)
  • Physical Goals: Money transferred to savings accounts
  • Virtual Goals: Money mentally allocated (still in checking)
  • Safe to Spend: Income - Expenses - Physical - Virtual

AI-Powered Insights

Premium users get personalized spending insights updated daily:

  • Spending trends and patterns
  • Budget recommendations
  • Unusual spending alerts
  • Savings opportunities

Family Member Filter

In family accounts, filter the dashboard to see:

  • All Family: Combined view of everyone's finances
  • Individual Member: Just one person's spending

üè¶ Accounts Management

Manage all your bank accounts, credit cards, and savings accounts in one place.

Adding an Account

  1. Click Accounts in navigation
  2. Click "+ Add Account"
  3. Fill in:
    • Account Name: e.g., "Chase Checking"
    • Account Type: Bank Account or Credit Card
    • Currency: USD, GBP, EUR, INR, etc.
    • Initial Balance & Date:
      • CRITICAL: Must match actual bank balance on the selected date.
      • Bank Accounts: Positive value (e.g., 5000) for assets.
      • Credit Cards: Negative value (e.g., -1200) for debt.
    • Budgeting Account Checkbox:
      • Check this for cash-flow accounts (Checking, Credit Cards).
      • Uncheck for investment or mortgage accounts.
  4. Click "Add Account"
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Accurate "Safe to Spend" relies on correct Initial Balances. Always use the Closing Balance from your bank statement for the date prior to your transaction history.

Account Types

  • Bank Account: Checking, savings, or money market accounts (Asset)
  • Credit Card: Credit card accounts (Liability/Debt)

Editing Accounts

Click the ‚úèÔ∏è icon next to any account to:

  • Change account name
  • Update current balance
  • Adjust initial balance and date

Deleting Accounts

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Deleting an account also deletes all its transactions. This cannot be undone!

üìù Transactions

Add, edit, and manage all your financial transactions.

Adding a Transaction Manually

  1. Click Add ‚Üí Transaction
  2. Select transaction type:
    • Expense: Money spent
    • Income: Money received
    • Internal Transfer: Moving money between your accounts
  3. Fill in details:
    • Date: Transaction date
    • Amount: Transaction amount
    • Account: Which account
    • Category: What type of expense/income
    • Note: Description
  4. Click "Add Transaction"

Internal Transfers

When moving money between your own accounts:

  • Select "Internal Transfer"
  • Choose source account (where money comes from)
  • Choose target account (where money goes)
  • Enter amount and date

The app creates two linked transactions automatically (debit and credit).

Editing Transactions

Click the ‚úèÔ∏è icon next to any transaction to modify it.

Deleting Transactions

Two ways to delete:

  • Single: Click ‚ùå next to transaction
  • Bulk: Check multiple transactions ‚Üí Click "Delete Selected"

Searching & Filtering

Use the Transaction Audit section to:

  • Search by description
  • Filter by category
  • Filter by account
  • Filter by date range

Recurring Transactions

Set up automatic monthly transactions:

  1. Click Add ‚Üí Recurring Transaction
  2. Fill in transaction details
  3. Set recurrence day (1-31)
  4. Choose status:
    • Active: Auto-creates transactions
    • Paused: Temporarily disabled

The app automatically creates transactions on the specified day each month.

ü§ñ AI Statement Upload (Premium)

Upload bank statements (subject to ~10 statements/mo fair usage) and let AI extract transactions automatically.

‚ö†Ô∏è Premium Feature: Requires Premium Solo or Premium Family subscription.

How It Works

  1. You upload a PDF or image of your bank statement (Max ~10 pages)
  2. Google Gemini AI "reads" the statement like a human
  3. Extracts all transactions with 95%+ accuracy
  4. Categorizes transactions automatically
  5. Detects duplicates to prevent double-counting
  6. You review and confirm

Uploading a Statement

  1. Click AI Statement Upload
  2. Click "Upload Statement"
  3. Select your account
  4. Upload PDF or image (JPG, PNG)
  5. Wait 10-30 seconds for processing
  6. Review extracted transactions
  7. Edit any incorrect categories
  8. Click "Confirm & Add All"

Validation & Scoring

‚ö†Ô∏è Requirement: Statement must have an Account Summary table (Opening/Closing Balance).
  • 100% Score: Perfect match (Opening Balance + Transactions = Closing Balance).
  • < 70% Score: Summary likely missing. Manually check transaction counts and categories.
  • Correction: If score is low, delete the statement and retry (ensure clear image). Max 3 attempts recommended.

Supported Formats

  • PDF statements (most common)
  • Image files (JPG, PNG)
  • Multi-page statements
  • Bank accounts and credit cards

Privacy & Security

  • Original PDF is permanently deleted after processing
  • Google does NOT store or train on your data
  • Only extracted transactions are saved

  • Spent: How much you've spent this month
  • Remaining: Budget left

Budget Warnings

The progress bar changes color:

  • Green: Under 70% of budget
  • Yellow: 70-90% of budget
  • Red: Over 90% or exceeded

üéØ Savings Goals

Set financial goals and track your progress automatically.

üí° Pro Tip: Keep your Goal Name the same as your bank transaction description (e.g., "Transfer to Aesop Settings"). This helps you identify them easily.

Physical vs Virtual Goals

Feature Physical Goal Virtual Goal
Money Location Separate savings account Still in checking account
Transfer Required Yes - you manually transfer No - mental allocation only
Safe to Spend Impact Reduces when transferred Reduces when allocated
Best For Long-term savings, emergency funds Short-term goals, bill savings

Creating a Savings Goal

  1. Go to Budget ‚Üí Savings Goals
  2. Click "+ Create Goal"
  3. Choose goal type (Physical or Virtual)
  4. Fill in:
    • Goal Name: e.g., "Emergency Fund"
    • Target Amount: Your goal
    • Per-Cycle Allocation: How much to save each pay cycle
    • Deduction Day: Which day of cycle to save (1-31)
  5. For Physical Goals: Select source and target accounts
  6. For Virtual Goals: Select source account
  7. Click "Create Goal"

Tracking Progress

The Savings Goals page shows:

  • Progress bar: Visual progress toward goal
  • Current amount: How much saved
  • Remaining: Amount left to reach goal
  • Next deduction date: When to save next
  • Transfer/Allocation status: Whether you've funded it this cycle

Physical Goal Actions

  • ‚ö° Fund Transfer: Log a manual transfer you made.
  • Important: If the transaction already exists in your uploaded Source Account statement (as a debit), uncheck the "Source Side" box. Only check "Target Side". This prevents duplicate transactions.
  • View transfer history

Virtual Goal Actions

  • ‚ö° Quick Fund: Allocate money to this goal.
  • Creates an internal transfer transaction automatically.
  • Reduces your "Safe to Spend" without moving real money.
  • View allocation history

Pausing Goals

Edit a goal and check "Pause Goal" to temporarily stop tracking without deleting it.
